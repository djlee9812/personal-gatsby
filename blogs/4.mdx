I'm always checking Google Flights and seats.aero for deals. Between signup bonuses and balances across airlines and hotels I probably have on the order of a million points to use up and I'm always looking for opportunities. 

2025 ended with trips across the Atlantic and a pile of convoluted routings. The first one was almost an accident: I was in Southern California for Christmas 2024 with no reasonable one-way back to Boston (direct flights were $300+). So I redeemed 47k Membership Rewards for SAS premium economy LA to Copenhagen and 45k Alaska miles for Shannon, Ireland to Boston in lie-flat business, and about 10 days across both countries. The routing was a bit silly-- I had 0 winter coats or warm pants, but the trip was amazing.

In March a friend pitched a Tanzania trip including Kilimanjaro, safari, and Zanzibar on KLM premium economy; I said yes. The ticket also earned ~2 Delta MQD per dollar. Later that fall I took off to Dubai with long layovers in Amsterdam and Paris for climbing, food, and shopping before continuing to Los Angeles-- roughly $1700 for over 4k MQD and qualified for Delta Gold Medallion. I spent United miles for Guanajuato and San Miguel de Allende (top rated domestic tourist destination within Mexico) when $250 round trips to Milan popped up leaving within days. May had Milan and central Mexico back to back.

The year ended with Jodi’s wedding in Shanghai. I first flew home to LA, spent a few nights in Osaka, then crossed into China for the ceremony. Leaving Asia I stitched Korean Air business into Incheon for 15k Flying Blue miles, another 15k Delta miles in business to Fukuoka, then JAL from Tokyo to Boston for 40k. Thirty-five days, both US coasts, three cities in Japan, two in China, and three weeks in Korea, mostly because award availability worked out.

I’m grateful for the flexibility to travel like this. I’ll save transit, meals, and climbing on real stone for another time; for now this is just how I got from A to B.
